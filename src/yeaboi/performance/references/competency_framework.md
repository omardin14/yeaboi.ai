# Engineering Competency Framework (default)

This is the default competency framework the 6-month review calibrates against
when the lead has not imported their own template. It is intentionally generic and
level-agnostic — a starting point a team can replace via
`PERFORMANCE_FRAMEWORK_PATH` or the Performance page's import action.

Evaluate the engineer across these dimensions, citing concrete evidence for each:

## 1. Technical Craft
- Writes correct, readable, maintainable code appropriate to the problem.
- Makes sound design/architecture trade-offs and explains them.
- Tests their work; considers edge cases, failure modes, and observability.
- Debugs effectively and gets to root causes.

## 2. Delivery & Execution
- Delivers committed work predictably; scopes and breaks down tasks well.
- Manages ambiguity and unblocks themselves; flags risks early.
- Balances speed and quality appropriately for the context.
- Estimates realistically and improves estimate accuracy over time.

## 3. Ownership & Impact
- Takes end-to-end ownership of features and their outcomes.
- Improves the systems and processes around them, not just assigned tickets.
- Drives measurable impact on the product, team, or codebase.

## 4. Collaboration & Communication
- Communicates clearly in writing (PRs, docs, tickets) and verbally.
- Gives and receives feedback constructively.
- Collaborates across disciplines; raises the team's effectiveness.
- Participates meaningfully in ceremonies (standups, retros, planning).

## 5. Growth & Reliability
- Seeks feedback and acts on it; demonstrates a growth trajectory.
- Is dependable — follows through on commitments and action items.
- Mentors or supports others as appropriate to their level.

## Rating guidance
For each dimension, assess whether the engineer is **below**, **meeting**, or
**exceeding** expectations for their role, and support the assessment with
specific examples from their delivery history, 1:1 records, and ceremony
participation. Prefer concrete evidence over adjectives.
